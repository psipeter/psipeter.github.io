You are invited to participate in a research study investigating how people learn to play a web-based investment game against various non-human and human opponents. The purpose of the study is to observe the strategies that naive and experienced players adopt when playing the game, with the long-term goal of understanding how the brain uses learning to make social decisions. What does participation involve?

As a participant in this study, you would be asked to fill out a short electronic survey (demographics, personality), then play an interactive game against software- and human-opponents. The game involves exchanging money with other players with the goal of maximizing personal profits. Your participation would involve at least 10 games and require approximately 30 minutes. Each move by the participant and the software agent will be recorded in an anonymous fashion. Upon completing all games, players will have the option to continue playing at their leisure, including against other human opponents who will be randomly chosen from the pool of players currently online.
